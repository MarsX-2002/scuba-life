Frequently Asked Questions

New to the world of scuba diving? Check out our FAQ section to find answers to the most common questions about scuba life, gear, training, and more. Get informed and get ready for your next adventure!

  • Do I need to be a good swimmer to scuba dive?

    While basic swimming skills are recommended, you don't need to be an expert swimmer to scuba dive. Scuba diving is all about breathing and buoyancy control, so as long as you feel comfortable in the water and can move around, you can learn to scuba dive.

  • Is scuba diving dangerous?

    Scuba diving can be a safe and enjoyable activity if you follow the rules and take proper precautions. Most scuba diving accidents happen due to human error or lack of training, so it's important to get certified and always dive with a buddy. Scuba diving also has some risks, such as decompression sickness, but these can be minimized by following safe diving practices and dive tables.

  • What equipment do I need to scuba dive?

    The basic scuba diving equipment includes a mask, fins, a regulator, a buoyancy compensator (BCD), and a tank. Some optional gear includes a wetsuit, a dive computer, and a dive light. You can rent or buy scuba gear at most dive shops.

  • How deep can I dive as a beginner?

    As a beginner scuba diver, you should start with shallow dives (around 12-18 meters/40-60 feet) and gradually progress to deeper dives as you gain experience and skills. The maximum depth for recreational scuba diving is 40 meters/130 feet, but most dives are shallower than that.

  • What should I do if I feel uncomfortable or anxious underwater?

    It's normal to feel a bit nervous or apprehensive when scuba diving, especially at the beginning. If you feel uncomfortable or anxious underwater, signal your buddy and ascend to shallower water or the surface. Taking a deep breath and focusing on your breathing can also help you relax.

  • Can I scuba dive if I wear glasses or contact lenses?

    Yes, you can wear glasses or contact lenses while scuba diving, but you'll need to use a special mask with prescription lenses or inserts. Many dive shops offer this service, and it's recommended to get your mask fitted and tested before your first dive. You can also wear contact lenses, but you should be aware of the risks of infection and irritation in the water.
